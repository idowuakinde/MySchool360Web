OPAL EYE

What is Opal Eye?

Opal Eye is a new revolutionary way to label clothing, bags and shoes that is quick and simple. It is successfully used in schools to label uniforms and other related items in seconds. It has the following merits;

  • No time consuming sewing
  • No ironing on adhesive labels
  • No unsightly names written in marker pen that washes off in the laundry process!

It is a small button that is etched into the garment’s owners name and is then attached to either the inside label, inside seam or on the garment using a simple hand held applicator. To remove the button, simply use the Applicator removal tool, remove the button and re-apply it to the new item of clothing. 

Opal Eye has been developed using material that will withstand industrial laundry temperatures and is suitable for use in the domestic and industrial washing processes as well as dry cleaning. 

- It has been ergonomically designed so that it is comfortable and barely noticeable to the wearer. Its design and size replicates a small spare button. It saves you time and provides a quick alternative to sew in and iron on labels and it won’t come off!

For more information contact the School Administrator
PASTORAL UPDATES

In line with recent medical research, the school has introduced an all new nutritionist inspired menu to aid growth and health among all the students. This menu operates on a two week cycle. Students are encouraged to report any problems associated with this new menu and make suggestions for any future review. All dining tables have new table clothes in a variety of colours.

Co-curricular activities now take place twice a week after school where the students are able to choose an activity from an extensive list including: Dance, Drama, Football, Basketball, Swimming, Karate, Chess, Art. These activities are supervised by qualified coaches with the

assistance of a member of staff. Coaches are vetted before access to the students. The school has plans to rotate these activities with the possibility of introducing additional options in the future.

The school has introduced electronic games consoles (Nintendo Wii and Playstation 3) and new flat screen televisions in both boys and girls dormitories. These games have an academic slant and access is restricted by the house parents.

The Multi-Purpose-Hall used for a variety of in-house activities. The facility is also used to introduce a fortnightly movie night for boarders. These movies are age specific and to date have included ‘Zoolander’ and ‘Hancock’.
